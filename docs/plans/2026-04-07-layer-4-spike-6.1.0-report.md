## 1. Civil baseline measurements

Three existing civil ranker prompts compared line-by-line by structural section (docstring, imports, system prompt by dimension, `SYSTEM_PROMPT` load, `ALLOWED_IMPACT_TARGETS`, `build_user_prompt`). Lines classified as **identical** (byte-for-byte same) / **vocab-only** (1-2 word substitution within the same sentence/structure) / **case-unique** (substantive content present in one file but absent or fundamentally different in another).

### File sizes
- `labor_dispute.py`: **208** lines
- `real_estate.py`: **215** lines
- `civil_loan.py`: **220** lines

### Pairwise reuse rates (identical + vocab-only) / max(file pair)

| Pair | Identical | Vocab-only | Case-unique | **Reuse %** | Strict identical % |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| labor_dispute ↔ real_estate | ~167 | ~13 | ~28 | **83.7%** | 77.7% |
| labor_dispute ↔ civil_loan | ~152 | ~13 | ~43 | **75.0%** | 69.1% |
| real_estate ↔ civil_loan | ~153 | ~14 | ~48 | **75.9%** | 69.5% |

### Where the case-unique mass concentrates

1. **Dim 2 `impact_targets` 说明 sub-bullets** — labor (5 lines) and real_estate (6 lines) explain their domain vocabulary inline; **civil_loan has no 说明 block at all**. Largest single source of pairwise divergence vs civil_loan.
2. **civil_loan dim 7 swing_score "关键区分" example block** (~7 lines) and dim 8/9 extra prose (~4 lines) — older verbose calibration not present in the Phase C.5b-aligned labor/real_estate templates.
3. **`ALLOWED_IMPACT_TARGETS` comment** — each cites a different legal authority (法释〔2025〕12号 / 法释〔2003〕7号 / civil_loan-specific). 6 lines case-unique per pair.
4. **civil_loan output-format prose** — extra "唯一合法的输出格式" sentence and a nested-example 禁止 line. ~2 lines.

**Implication**: labor_dispute and real_estate (both touched by Phase C.5b) sit on a tighter shared template. civil_loan is the outlier. A pre-spike cleanup pass aligning civil_loan to the labor/real_estate template (estimated ~15 LOC delta) would push **all three pairs above 80% reuse**. This is a free win for §"问题 5" credibility.

### Headline result vs the 60% gate

| Metric | Threshold | Measured | Verdict |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Min pairwise reuse (incl. vocab-only) | ≥ 60% | **75.0%** | ✅ pass with margin |
| Min pairwise strict-identical | (no threshold) | 69.1% | informational |
| All pairs ≥ 60% | yes | yes | ✅ |

The civil corpus alone **already clears the gate**. This is the strongest signal we will get without actually running 6.1.0 against criminal — the civil family has had enough independent evolution (labor_dispute is the newest, civil_loan the oldest) that the residual structural overlap is a fair lower bound for a fresh family designed with inheritance in mind.

## 2. Criminal base + intentional_injury override (hand-written)

Full source in **`/tmp/spike/intentional_injury_ranker_prompt.md`** (artifact, not installed).

### Architectural shape
- `_criminal_base.py` defines `CRIMINAL_BASE_SYSTEM_PROMPT_TEMPLATE` (string with 7 named placeholders) + `build_criminal_system_prompt()` helper + family-level `build_criminal_user_prompt()`.
- `intentional_injury.py` provides only:
  1. `ALLOWED_IMPACT_TARGETS` frozenset (5 entries — 4 criminal-specific + `credibility` neutral pivot)
  2. `_IMPACT_TARGETS_GLOSSARY` string (6 lines, anchored to 刑法 234 + 人体损伤程度鉴定标准 GB/T 18667-2002)
  3. `_SUBTYPE_CALIBRATION_HINT` (3 lines — 伤情/部位/工具/故意/因果关系 score bands)
  4. `_SUBTYPE_SCORE_BAND_HINT` (1 line)
  5. `_SUBTYPE_HARD_CONSTRAINTS` (4 lines — rules tying伤情鉴定意见 evidence to importance_score floors)
  6. Two example string constants for the JSON contract block
  7. One `build_criminal_system_prompt(...)` call wiring it all together
  8. Thin `build_user_prompt(...)` wrapper that delegates to family helper, overriding only `verdict_block_hint_targets`

### Line counts
- `_criminal_base.py`: **~190 lines** (135-line template body + ~55 lines builders/imports/header). Written once per family per engine.
- `intentional_injury.py`: **~62 lines**. Pure additive parameterization — zero subtraction.

### Override rate

| Quantity | Lines |
|---|---:|
| Family base | 190 |
| Subtype override | 62 |
| **Subtype override rate** = 62 / (190 + 62) | **24.6%** |
| **First-subtype reuse rate** = 190 / 252 | **75.4%** |

### Comparison: labor_dispute as strawman base (Scenario A) vs purpose-built criminal base (Scenario B)

| Property | Scenario A (labor as base) | Scenario B (criminal base) |
|---|---|---|
| Override LOC | ~45 (subtract + add) | **62 (pure add)** |
| Headline reuse % | 82% | 75.4% |
| Subtraction required? | YES (~30 lines of labor vocab) | NO |
| Leak risk on base edits? | HIGH (labor edits silently leak into criminal) | NONE (template is family-neutral by construction) |
| Maintenance cost over time | grows with every labor change | constant |
| **Verdict** | Headline number is misleading | **Strategic choice** |

**Key finding**: the headline "reuse %" is necessary but not sufficient. **Override structure (additive vs subtractive) matters more than the percent**. Scenario A scores higher on the gate but is operationally fragile; Scenario B scores lower but is the only structure that scales safely past one subtype.

## 3. Cost curves: flat per-subtype vs 2-layer inheritance

**Inputs**: flat ≈ 210 LOC/subtype (close to existing civil files); 2-layer = 190 + 62k where k = subtype count per engine.

### Per engine (one engine, one family, k criminal subtypes)

| k | Flat | 2-layer | Savings | Effective reuse |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 1 | 210 | 252 | **−42 (worse)** | n/a |
| 2 | 420 | 314 | 25.2% | 60.5% |
| 3 (intentional_injury / theft / fraud — Layer 4 MVP) | 630 | 376 | **40.3%** | 79.5% |
| 5 | 1,050 | 500 | 52.4% | 86.0% |
| 10 | 2,100 | 810 | 61.4% | 92.4% |

**Breakeven**: k=2. Below that, inheritance loses to flat. Layer 4 plans k≥3 from day one in 6.1+6.2.

### Across all 17 ranker-style engines (Layer 4 MVP scope)

| Strategy | LOC (criminal family only) | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Flat per-subtype × 17 engines × 3 subtypes | 17 × 3 × 210 = **10,710** | v1's implied baseline |
| 2-layer × 17 engines (one base each) × 3 subtypes | 17 × (190 + 3×62) = **6,392** | proposed |
| **Saving** | **−4,318 LOC (≈40%)** | grows with every new subtype |

For the full criminal+admin scope (3 criminal + 3 admin subtypes × 17 engines × 2 family bases):
- Flat: 17 × 6 × 210 = 21,420 LOC
- 2-layer: 17 × (2 × 190 + 6 × 62) = 17 × 752 = 12,784 LOC
- Saving: ~8,600 LOC (~40%)

These numbers are still rough — they ignore (a) per-engine variance in base size (e.g. `defense_chain` will likely have a heavier criminal base than `issue_impact_ranker`), (b) engines that don't need a family base at all (already case-type-neutral, ~3-5 of the 17), (c) few-shot file LOC (not counted here, scales with subtype count regardless of strategy). But the **40% direction is robust** to all these adjustments.

## 4. Verdict

**OK with one tweak.** The 2-layer inheritance strategy is approved for Batch 6.1+ on three converging signals:

1. **Civil baseline reuse already clears the 60% gate** at 75-84% pairwise (worst pair: 75.0%). This is empirical, not modeled.
2. **The hand-written criminal base + intentional_injury override is purely additive** — 62 LOC override on 190 LOC base, zero subtraction, zero leak risk. The override structure scales safely.
3. **Cost curves cross flat at k=2** and reach 40% savings at k=3 (the Layer 4 MVP target). This is well past the noise floor.

### The tweak (mandatory before 6.1.1)

Add a **6.1.0b cleanup task**: align `civil_loan.py` to the Phase C.5b-shaped labor_dispute / real_estate template. Specifically:
- Add a `## 说明` sub-block to dim 2 (currently absent) for parity with the other two civil prompts.
- Trim the dim 7 "关键区分" example block (~7 lines) and the dim 8/9 extra prose down to the labor/real_estate-shaped form.
- Trim the output-format extra prose (~2 lines).

This brings all three civil files onto the same template, raises pairwise civil reuse to >80%, and — critically — gives the criminal base a **unified civil exemplar** to copy structural decisions from. Without this, the criminal base inherits civil's internal inconsistency. Estimated effort: **<1 day**, no behavioral test changes (Phase C.5b's few-shot vocabulary stays put).

### What was NOT validated by this spike

- Whether **other engines** (especially `case_extractor`, `defense_chain`, `pretrial_conference/judge`) have civil baselines that would also clear 60%. Plan §"问题 5" warns these may degenerate. **Recommendation**: re-run this spike methodology against `case_extractor` as 6.1.0c before committing to the 17-engine rollout. ~30 min of work; significantly de-risks 6.2.
- Whether the criminal base actually produces correct LLM output. This spike is a structural measurement only; **6.1.4 smoke test + golden case is still the binding gate** for behavioral correctness.
- Few-shot file reuse rates — not in scope for 6.1.0, but worth a follow-up spike before 6.2.

### What this spike rules OUT

- The "labor_dispute as informal base" shortcut (Scenario A above). Even though it scores higher on the headline reuse %, the subtractive override structure is operationally unsafe. Scenario B is the only viable path forward.
- v1's blanket "~80% compression" claim. Realistic compression at MVP scope (3 subtypes/family, 17 engines) is ~40% LOC, not 80%. Still worth doing — but the timeline implication is real.
